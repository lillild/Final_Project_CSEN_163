Simple Omelette

Uses from your pantry: egg, onion

You’d also need: cheese, butter

How to make it

  1. Crack eggs into a bowl, add a pinch of salt and pepper, and beat with a fork until the mixture is smooth and slightly frothy.
  2. Heat butter (and a little oil if using) in a non-stick pan over medium heat. Add chopped onion and cook until softened and fragrant.
  3. Pour the beaten eggs over the onions. Let the eggs set around the edges, gently pushing the cooked parts toward the center so the uncooked egg flows outward.
  4. When the omelette is mostly set but still slightly soft on top, sprinkle cheese over one half. Fold the omelette over the filling and cook another 30–60 seconds until the cheese melts. Slide onto a plate and serve immediately.
